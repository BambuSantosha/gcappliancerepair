The Honest Answer

In Most Cases,
Repair Wins.

The appliance industry has a financial incentive to get you buying new. We don't. Gold Country Appliance Repair makes money by fixing things, not by telling you to throw them away — which means when we tell you something isn't worth fixing, you can trust it.

The general rule is simple: if the appliance is less than halfway through its expected lifespan and the repair cost is less than half of replacement cost, repair is almost always the smarter financial decision.

We will always give you a clear diagnosis and honest quote before any work begins. If a repair doesn't make sense, we'll tell you that too.

The Simple Framework

Repair vs. Replace:
How to Decide

Lean Toward Repair When...

  • The appliance is less than halfway through its expected lifespan
  • The repair cost is less than 50% of a comparable new unit
  • The appliance is a premium or high-end brand (Sub-Zero, Wolf, Miele, Thermador)
  • The problem is mechanical — a part that failed, not systemic deterioration
  • A new unit would require modifications to fit your space
  • You're happy with the appliance's performance otherwise

Lean Toward Replace When...

  • The appliance is near or past its expected lifespan
  • The repair cost exceeds 50-75% of replacement cost
  • Multiple systems are failing at once
  • Parts are no longer available or must be shipped internationally
  • Energy inefficiency is costing you significantly month over month
  • The same problem has recurred multiple times
