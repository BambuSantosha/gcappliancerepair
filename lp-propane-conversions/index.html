Why LP Propane Conversions

Does Your Home
Run on Propane?

Thousands of homes throughout Nevada County, Placer County, and the Sierra Nevada foothills rely on liquid propane (LP) rather than natural gas. Rural properties, mountain communities, and many foothill subdivisions are simply not connected to the natural gas grid.

When you purchase a new appliance or move into a home with a different fuel setup, a proper LP propane conversion is essential. This is not a DIY job — it requires precise orifice resizing specific to each appliance model, correct regulator adjustment, and leak testing after installation.

Gold Country Appliance Repair performs LP propane conversions throughout Nevada County and Placer County, including rural communities like Penn Valley, Rough and Ready, North San Juan, Cascade Shores, Colfax, Alta, and Dutch Flat.

⚠️ Why Proper LP Conversion Matters

Natural gas and liquid propane burn at different pressures and require different orifice sizes. An incorrect or incomplete conversion can cause dangerous gas leaks, carbon monoxide buildup, inefficient burning, or appliance damage. Gold Country Appliance Repair uses the correct manufacturer-specified conversion kits for each appliance model and performs full leak testing after every conversion. Never attempt an LP conversion yourself or hire an uncertified technician.
